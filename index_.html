The objective of the project "Media monitoring of the past. Mining 200 years of historical newspapers" is to enable critical text mining of newspaper archives with the implementation of a technological framework to extract, process, link, and explore data from print media archives. Supported by an interdisciplinary consortium composed of computational linguists, digital humanists, designers, historians, librarians and archivists, impresso (i.e. “what has been printed”) tackled the challenges of content enrichment and data representation, visualization and analysis, completed by methodological and epistemological reflections. Our outcomes include, among others, a set of natural language processing (NLP) tools and data dedicated to historical print media, a user interface for active exploration and critical analysis of newspaper corpora and accompanying didactic materials.

Time frame: September 2017 - December 2020

Funding: Swiss National Science Foundation
